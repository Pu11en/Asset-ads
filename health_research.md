## MANGO (*Mangifera indica*)
**Source: USDA FoodData Central, Healthline, Journal of Nutrition**

### Nutrition (per 1 cup / 165g fresh mango)
- **Calories:** 99
- **Vitamin C:** 67% Daily Value (DV) — supports immune system, collagen production, iron absorption
- **Vitamin A:** 10% DV — from beta-carotene; supports vision, skin health, immune function
- **Folate:** 18% DV — essential for cell growth, DNA synthesis, pregnancy health
- **Vitamin B6:** 15% DV — brain development, neurotransmitter synthesis
- **Copper:** 20% DV — red blood cell formation, iron metabolism
- **Fiber:** 2.6g — digestive health, gut microbiome support
- **Potassium:** 6% DV — electrolyte balance, heart rhythm

### Key Compounds
- **Beta-carotene** — antioxidant, converts to Vitamin A
- **Mangiferin** — polyphenol with anti-inflammatory, neuroprotective properties (studied for metabolic health)
- **Amylases** — digestive enzymes that break down carbs and starches

### Research-Backed Benefits
1. **Immune support** — high Vitamin C + Vitamin A strengthen immune response
2. **Digestive health** — fiber + digestive enzymes support gut motility and nutrient absorption
3. **Eye health** — Vitamin A + beta-carotene support retinal function, may reduce macular degeneration risk
4. **Skin health** — collagen synthesis support from Vitamin C; beta-carotene may protect against UV damage
5. **Anti-inflammatory** — mangiferin and other polyphenols reduce inflammatory markers

## PASSION FRUIT (*Passiflora edulis*)
**Source: USDA FoodData Central, Journal of Food Science**

### Nutrition (per 1 cup / 165g fresh passion fruit)
- **Fiber:** 10.4g — 37% DV — one of the highest fiber contents of any fruit
- **Vitamin C:** 30% DV — antioxidant, immune support
- **Vitamin A:** 25% DV — from beta-carotene
- **Folate:** 15% DV — cell growth, pregnancy health
- **Iron:** 12% DV — oxygen transport in blood
- **Potassium:** 7% DV — heart and muscle function
- **Riboflavin (B2):** 11% DV — energy production

### Key Compounds
- **Flavonoids** (vitexin, quercetin, apigenin) — antioxidants with anti-anxiety, anti-inflammatory properties
- **Harman alkaloids** — studied for calming/relaxing effects
- **Cyanogenic glycosides** — in seeds only; not in juice

### Research-Backed Benefits
1. **Digestive health** — exceptional fiber content (mostly insoluble) promotes regularity
2. **Anxiety/relaxation** — flavonoids may have GABA-modulating effects; traditional use for calming
3. **Blood sugar control** — fiber slows glucose absorption; polyphenols improve insulin sensitivity
4. **Anti-inflammatory** — flavonoids reduce inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6)
5. **Heart health** — fiber + potassium + antioxidants support cardiovascular function
6. **Immune support** — Vitamin C + Vitamin A + antioxidants

## MAUBY (*Colubrina elliptica*)
**Source: Caribbean traditional medicine, Journal of Ethnopharmacology**

### Traditional Caribbean Uses
Mauby is a bitter bark traditionally made into a beverage in the Caribbean (Jamaica, Trinidad, Bahamas). Used for centuries as a digestive tonic and general wellness drink.

### Key Properties
- **Bitter principles** — stimulate digestive secretions (bile, gastric juices)
- **Saponins** — natural surfactants that aid digestion
- **Tannins** — anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial
- **Flavonoids** — antioxidant activity

### Research-Backed Benefits
1. **Digestive stimulation** — bitter compounds trigger digestive enzyme release (traditional apéritif use)
2. **Liver support** — bitters support Phase I and Phase II detoxification pathways
3. **Metabolism boost** — bitter flavors may increase metabolic rate slightly
4. **Appetite regulation** — traditional use as appetite stimulant or suppressor depending on preparation
5. **Blood sugar** — some studies suggest bitter botanicals support glucose metabolism

## PEANUT (*Arachis hypogaea*) — Peanut Punch
**Source: USDA FoodData Central, American Journal of Clinical Nutrition**

### Nutrition (per 1oz / 28g raw peanuts ~½ cup punch serving)
- **Protein:** 7.3g — muscle building, satiety
- **Healthy Fats:** 14g (mostly monounsaturated — oleic acid)
- **Vitamin E:** 21% DV — antioxidant, protects cell membranes
- **Niacin (B3):** 23% DV — energy metabolism, cholesterol management
- **Folate (B9):** 17% DV — cell growth, pregnancy health
- **Magnesium:** 12% DV — muscle/nerve function, blood sugar control
- **Phosphorus:** 13% DV — bone health, energy production
- **Copper:** 25% DV — iron metabolism, connective tissue, neurotransmitter synthesis
- **Manganese:** 55% DV — antioxidant defenses, bone development

### Key Compounds
- **Resveratrol** — polyphenol with anti-aging, cardiovascular protective effects
- **Genistein/Daidzin** — isoflavones with phytoestrogenic activity
- **Beta-sitosterol** — plant sterol that lowers LDL cholesterol
- **Arginine** — amino acid that converts to nitric oxide; vasodilator

### Research-Backed Benefits
1. **Heart health** — monounsaturated fats + resveratrol + beta-sitosterol reduce cardiovascular disease risk; FDA qualified health claim for nuts and heart disease
2. **Blood sugar control** — magnesium + fiber + low carbs support insulin sensitivity
3. **Brain health** — niacin (B3) supports cognitive function; resveratrol may cross blood-brain barrier
4. **Muscle recovery** — protein + magnesium support muscle repair after exercise
5. **Satiety/weight management** — protein + healthy fats promote fullness
6. **Energy production** — B vitamins + magnesium + manganese cofactors for energy metabolism

## LIME (*Citrus aurantifolia*)
**Source: USDA FoodData Central, Journal of Food Science**

### Nutrition (per 1 medium lime / 67g)
- **Vitamin C:** 32% DV — antioxidant, immune, collagen synthesis
- **Flavonoids:** (hesperidin, naringenin, limonin) — antioxidant, anti-inflammatory
- **Potassium:** 3% DV
- **Folate:** 2% DV
- **Calcium:** 2% DV

### Key Compounds
- **Limonoids** (limonin, nomilin) — unique to citrus; studied for anti-cancer, antimicrobial properties
- **Flavonoid glycosides** — antioxidant, anti-inflammatory
- **Citric acid** — gives sour taste; mild antimicrobial
- **Limonene** — in peel oil; studied for cholesterol-lowering effects

### Research-Backed Benefits
1. **Immune boost** — very high Vitamin C supports white blood cell function
2. **Antioxidant defense** — Vitamin C + flavonoids neutralize free radicals
3. **Kidney stone prevention** — citric acid may help prevent calcium oxalate stones
4. **Digestive aid** — sour taste stimulates digestive enzymes; traditional morning lime water practice
5. **Anti-inflammatory** — flavonoids reduce systemic inflammation
6. **Iron absorption** — Vitamin C enhances non-heme iron absorption from plant foods
7. **Oral health** — antimicrobial properties of citric acid and flavonoids; traditional use for gum health

## GUAVA (*Psidium guajava*)
**Source: USDA FoodData Central, Journal of Food and Nutrition Research**

### Nutrition (per 1 cup / 165g fresh guava)
- **Vitamin C:** 377% DV — ONE OF THE HIGHEST VITAMIN C SOURCES IN THE WORLD; 4x more than oranges
- **Vitamin A:** 21% DV — from beta-carotene
- **Folate:** 40% DV — cell growth and development
- **Fiber:** 9g — 32% DV — supports digestive health
- **Potassium:** 21% DV — electrolyte balance, blood pressure regulation
- **Manganese:** 11% DV
- **Copper:** 11% DV

### Key Compounds
- **Lycopene** — in pink/red guava; powerful antioxidant (more than tomatoes in some studies)
- **Quercetin** — flavonoid with strong anti-inflammatory effects
- **Gallic acid** — polyphenol with antioxidant, antimicrobial properties

### Research-Backed Benefits
1. **Immune powerhouse** — Vitamin C at 377% DV; supports white blood cell production, antibody function
2. **Heart health** — fiber + potassium + antioxidants reduce LDL oxidation, support healthy blood pressure
3. **Blood sugar control** — fiber slows glucose absorption; low glycemic index
4. **Digestive health** — exceptional fiber for gut motility and microbiome diversity
5. **Anti-aging/antioxidant** — Vitamin C + lycopene + quercetin neutralize free radicals
6. **Eye health** — Vitamin A + beta-carotene support retinal function
7. **Cancer risk reduction** — lycopene and quercetin have shown anti-proliferative effects on cancer cells in studies

## PINEAPPLE (*Ananas comosus*)
**Source: USDA FoodData Central, Journal of Agricultural and Food Chemistry**

### Nutrition (per 1 cup / 165g fresh pineapple)
- **Vitamin C:** 131% DV — antioxidant, immune
- **Manganese:** 109% DV — antioxidant enzyme cofactor, bone health
- **Vitamin B6:** 9% DV
- **Folate:** 7% DV
- **Fiber:** 2.3g — 8% DV
- **Potassium:** 5% DV
- **Copper:** 10% DV

### Key Compounds
- **Bromelain** — proteolytic enzyme complex unique to pineapple; anti-inflammatory, digestive aid
- **Phenolic compounds** (chlorogenic acid, ferulic acid) — antioxidants
- **Beta-carotene** — antioxidant precursor to Vitamin A

### Research-Backed Benefits
1. **Digestive enzyme** — bromelain breaks down proteins; supports protein digestion and nutrient absorption
2. **Anti-inflammatory** — bromelain reduces inflammation; studied for sinusitis, arthritis, sports injuries
3. **Immune support** — Vitamin C (131% DV) + bromelain modulate immune response
4. **Wound healing** — bromelain + Vitamin C support tissue repair
5. **Sinus/allergy relief** — bromelain reduces nasal mucus and inflammation
6. **Heart health** — bromelain may reduce blood clot formation; potassium supports blood pressure
7. **Bone health** — manganese + Vitamin C + copper support collagen and bone matrix

## SORREL (*Hibiscus sabdariffa*)
**Source: USDA FoodData Central, Journal of Nutrition, Phytomedicine**

### Nutrition (per 1 cup brewed tea)
- **Vitamin C:** moderate — antioxidant
- **Anthocyanins** — powerful antioxidants giving deep red color (delphinidin, cyanidin)
- **Organic acids** (hibiscus, citric, malic) — antimicrobial properties
- **Polysaccharides** — immune-supporting fibers

### Key Compounds
- **Anthocyanins** — among the highest of any edible plant; 5-10x more than red wine
- **Hibiscus acid** — unique to sorrel; studied for metabolic effects
- **Polyphenols** (quercetin, gallic acid) — antioxidant, anti-inflammatory
- **Beta-carotene** — precursor to Vitamin A

### Research-Backed Benefits
1. **Blood pressure reduction** — multiple human trials show hibiscus tea significantly lowers both systolic and diastolic BP (comparable to some medications); mechanism: ACE inhibition, diuretic effect
2. **Liver protection** — anthocyanins protect liver cells from oxidative damage; improves liver enzyme markers
3. **Cholesterol management** — studies show hibiscus reduces LDL ("bad") cholesterol and triglycerides; increases HDL
4. **Anti-obesity** — hibiscus inhibits digestive enzyme (amylase); reduces carbohydrate absorption
5. **Antioxidant** — anthocyanins neutralize free radicals; ORAC values among highest for teas
6. **Anti-inflammatory** — polyphenols reduce inflammatory markers (CRP, TNF-alpha)
7. **Antibacterial** — organic acids inhibit growth of E. coli, Salmonella, H. pylori

### Traditional Caribbean Use
Traditional Christmas/New Year's drink in Caribbean. Served cold spiced with ginger, cloves, sometimes rum. Believed to support liver, circulation, and ward off illness after holiday indulgences.

## GINGER (*Zingiber officinale*)
**Source: USDA FoodData Central, Journal of Medicinal Food, Phytotherapy Research**

### Nutrition (per 1 tablespoon / 6g fresh ginger)
- **Potassium:** 5% DV
- **Magnesium:** 3% DV
- **Vitamin B6:** 3% DV
- **Manganese:** 3% DV
- **Vitamin C:** 1% DV
- **Fiber:** minimal but bioactive compounds abundant

### Key Compounds
- **Gingerols** (especially 6-gingerol) — primary bioactive; anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, anti-nausea
- **Shogaols** — formed when ginger is dried/cooked; more potent antioxidant
- **Zingerone** — from gingerol breakdown; studied for anti-diarrheal effects
- **Volatile oils** — responsible for aroma; antimicrobial properties
- **Paradols** — from gingerol hydrogenation; anti-inflammatory, anti-cancer

### Research-Backed Benefits
1. **Nausea/vomiting relief** — strongest evidence for pregnancy-related nausea (morning sickness) and chemotherapy-induced nausea; works on 5-HT3 receptors in gut
2. **Anti-inflammatory** — gingerols inhibit COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes (like NSAIDs but milder); effective for osteoarthritis, muscle soreness
3. **Digestive health** — accelerates gastric emptying, reduces dyspepsia, relieves bloating and cramping
4. **Blood sugar control** — improves insulin sensitivity; studies show fasting glucose reduction
5. **Pain reduction** — anti-inflammatory reduces menstrual pain (dysmenorrhea); comparable to ibuprofen in some studies
6. **Antimicrobial** — volatile oils inhibit bacteria (including H. pylori) and fungi
7. **Immune support** — anti-inflammatory + antimicrobial; traditional use for colds, sore throat
8. **Anti-cancer potential** — in vitro studies show gingerol/shogaol inhibit GI cancer cell growth
